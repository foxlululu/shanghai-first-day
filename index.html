Shanghai First Day Guide

A survival guide for foreigners in their first 24 hours in Shanghai

Before You Arrive

  • Valid passport + visa (or visa-free eligibility)
  • Hotel address written in English & Chinese
  • International bank card (Visa / Mastercard)
  • Install: Alipay / WeChat / Didi / Google Translate

Arrival at Airport

  1. Immigration control
  2. Baggage claim
  3. SIM card / eSIM setup
  4. Airport Wi-Fi connection
  5. Currency exchange (optional)
Tip: Follow “Exit / Ground Transportation” signs.

Getting to the City

  • Taxi / Didi — Recommended for first-time visitors
  • Metro — Cheapest option, requires navigation
  • Airport Bus — Budget but slower

Payment in China

  • Alipay (supports international cards)
  • WeChat Pay (widely used)
  • Cash as backup
If payment fails, try another app or ask for cash option.

Must-have Apps

  • Didi (transport)
  • Amap / Apple Maps (navigation)
  • Google Translate (offline pack)
  • Trip.com (travel booking)
  • Meituan (food delivery)

First 24 Hours Route

  • Morning: The Bund
  • Afternoon: Yu Garden
  • Evening: Wukang Road / Xintiandi

Emergency Cards

I can’t pay → “Do you accept cash?”
Need taxi → “Please take me to this address.”
No internet → “My mobile data is not working.”
Need hospital → “I need to go to a hospital.”
Lost passport → “I lost my passport.”
